Bridesmaids

Happy Bridesmaids make a happy Bride

Emily Chan
Maid of Honor
Emily and Maggie have been inseparable since they first played together in day care as 5-year-olds. By fate, Emily and Maggie were neighbors across the street, and not only attended the same schools from Kindergarten to 12th grade, but also Chinese school on Saturdays. Often mistaken as sisters, Emily was the Yin to Maggie’s Yang as they complemented each other throughout the years. To this day, Emily will gladly continue to share the earbuds to her iPod Mini, do party quests together in Maplestory, go on foodie and boba adventures, binge-watch “Friends” for the 10th time, and someday rejoin the gym together with Maggie. Emily looks forward to experiencing many more important milestones in life with Maggie and to continue seeing Maggie’s beautiful and contagious smile.
Tina Tran
Bridesmaid
Tina and Maggie first met in the cafeteria during their first year of middle school. Being a freshman student struggling to make friends in a new environment, Tina is forever grateful for that first encounter with the dynamic duo, Emily and Maggie. From then on, it was after school adventures for Quickly’s milk tea and popcorn chicken everyday! Through the years, Maggie’s amusing sense of humor, cravings for good food, love for all things boba, and admirable work ethic has inspired Tina to always live her best life.
Susan Tang
Bridesmaid
Like many of the other bridesmaids, Susan and Maggie met during their middle school days over a decade ago. While sharing classes allowed them to become acquainted with one another, lunch time in the Roosevelt Middle School cafeteria was when their friendship started to develop. As they moved onto high school, they shared a love for the Korean pop band SHINee, both fangirling over the same favorite member. Susan can always count on Maggie for food adventures, pragmatic advice, and a smile to brighten up her day, from the start of their relationship 14 years ago to the future days of their everlasting friendship.
Susanna Zhang
Bridesmaid
Maggie was born two days before Susanna and met when they were both 13 years old at Roosevelt Middle School. Everyday after class, they would quickly finish their homework and sneak out to Clement Street for food and boba. Both 4.0 students, Susanna and Maggie got accepted into Lowell High School and continued to be best friends. Although they had no classes together, they would always spend time with each other during lunch breaks, on the weekends, and during holiday breaks. After high school, Susanna chose to study accounting at UCSB and Maggie went on to study nursing at USF. This was the first time they were 300 miles away, but stayed close updating each other about school, new friendships, and relationships. To this day, they are best friends and Susanna always goes to Maggie for advice, as she always has her life together. We love Maggie for her caring personality and will always love her as an older sister.
Groomsmen

I Found My Woman But I Still Need My Boys

Kingsley Kuang
Best Man
Kingsley met Steven in the fall of 2007, when they both were budding civil engineers at UC Berkeley. It did not take long for these two San Francisco natives to form a bond together. It was this bond that carried them through rough, but memorable times in Berkeley. Kingsley “followed” Steven into the professional world by interning at the same company and attending grad school together at San Jose State. Through the years, Kingsley and Steven have stayed close and were there for each other for momentous life events: housewarmings, weddings, and kids. But when times are not as serious, these two still have time for a couple rounds of Street Fighter or Smash Bros. Now if only they can get their acts together and start hitting the gym again…
Irving Chang
Groomsman
Irving and Steven have known each other since they were very young. They consider each other as hing dai (兄弟) or brothers because of their long history as friends. Even with their difference in age, they have bonded over their love in gaming, eating and finding deals. They would spend many weekends scrounging through their local video game store for the best game discs and cartridges, spending all night laughing and playing through them together. Both would also relax and chat over a large bowl of Phở noodles at the local Vietnamese restaurant after a tough workout at the gym. Through the pivotal changes that went on in their lives, they still find time to enjoy their youthful passions and bond over games and foods.
Kevin Lim
Groomsman
As cousins living two blocks away, Kevin and Steven would always hang out together. They kept each other motivated every weekend to go swimming or hit the gym early in the morning. They continuously took on new activities such as bouldering, the kickbox cardio class at the gym, and more. Kevin would meet Steven at the gym after class in high school to lift weights and catch the Zumba class where Irving, Emily and Maggie would later join. To this day, Steven’s strong drive and ambition inspires Kevin to be the best version of himself. Kevin will forever be thankful to Steven for being the older brother and role model he never had.

Groomsman
